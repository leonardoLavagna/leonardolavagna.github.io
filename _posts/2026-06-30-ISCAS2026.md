We recently presented two contributions at the [2026 IEEE International Symposium on Circuits and Systems (ISCAS)](https://2026.ieee-iscas.org/) in Shangai.
These contributions are a natural continuation of our works about time-space trade-offs in quantum cryptosystems (I've talked about this
research topic in a couple of blog posts, se e.g. [here](https://lavagnaleo.wordpress.com/2025/02/28/hellmans-construction-and-quantum-cryptography/)). In this case we focused
on two complementary aspects of the quantum version of the "Hellman construction" proposed last year at the same conference in London 
(cf., [this post](https://leonardolavagna.github.io/posts/2025/5/materials-ISCAS2025/) and [this paper](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11043205/))
- [A paper](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11043205/) about a unified framework for cryptographyc search based on quantum walks and grover's algorithm
- [A paper](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11562218 about the impossibility of heaving multiplicative resource scaling in cryptographic search under the previous unified model.

More materials (including the code) are available [here](https://lavagnaleo.wordpress.com/2026/02/26/follow-up-on-the-hellmans-construction-and-quantum-cryptography/)
and [here](https://github.com/leonardoLavagna/Iscas2026)
